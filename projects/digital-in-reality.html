Digital in Reality

Mixed media installation · Projection and materials · Perception, texture, and light

Introduction

This project is a journey through my exploration of perception, materiality, and digital interaction. My work investigates how textures, light, and form can transform digital experiences when integrated into physical spaces.

By experimenting with projections, layered surfaces, and interactive elements, I aim to create environments where the digital and the real coexist, offering new ways to observe, interpret, and engage with the world around us.

Creating media

Mixed media installations and interactive digital works built with:

  • Tools: Unity, Maya, Cinema 4D, Adobe Substance 3D Painter
  • Materials: fabric, metal foil, glass, and magnetic levitation mechanisms

Digital world in reality

Previously, I created a fully digital game in Unity, which led me to wonder how it could exist in the physical world—where people engage their senses beyond the screen.

Projection became a bridge between digital content and physical reality. On my advisor's suggestion, I began projecting onto different textures to create varied visual effects. Because each material reflects light differently, the projection distorts in unique ways, transforming the digital scene into new visual experiences in physical space.

Optical variations across surfaces

The project studies how light behaves on different materials:

  • Light reflection — Smooth surfaces create clearer images; rough surfaces scatter light.
  • Light scattering — Rough textures lead to distortion, graininess, or blur.
  • Texture absorption — Fibrous textures absorb more light, darkening the image.
  • Light transmission — Transparent materials soften, fade, or double the projected image.

I experimented with white blanket, white wall, fabric, sheer mesh, metal foil, and glass.

Magnetic levitation & Rubik's Cube motif

A magnetic levitation installation adds dynamic movement beside a glass cube. The cube resembles a Rubik's Cube—the central motif of my game—strengthening the conceptual and spatial coherence of the project.

Innovating perception through texture and light

I see the digital and the real as complementary. My installation suggests that the digital does not replace the real, but enriches it, offering new ways to see and interpret the world.

Installation design

Layering different textures allows the digital scene to interact with the physical surfaces. The installation presents "worlds through different eyes"— juxtaposing how a human might see with how a snake, cow, horse, fish, pigeon, or fly might perceive the same environment.
