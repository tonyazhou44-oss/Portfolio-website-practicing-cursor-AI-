Oblivisci

Collaborative VR game · Mental health narrative · Spatial interaction

Introduction

Oblivisci is a 3D VR horror game created as a collaborative project. Another teammate and I handled the art, while other team members focused on programming and sound design. The game was one of three concepts pitched to our TAs and professors; Oblivisci was selected and developed into a full experience.

The game follows a psychological journey into mental illness. Players complete simple, everyday chores in what appears to be a normal home. As they progress, subtle anomalies begin to appear—a disappearing apple, small visual shifts—slowly escalating into more unsettling events, particularly after entering the basement. Over time, the player realizes they are experiencing symptoms of mental illness.

My part

My responsibilities focused on 3D art, texturing, and spatial layout inside Unity:

  • Modeling in Maya
  • Original concept art for the environment and mood
  • Texturing in Adobe Substance 3D Painter
  • Arranging rooms and props in Unity

From asylum cliché to ordinary home

Our team initially wanted to set the story in an asylum. However, our TAs pointed out that this setting can be cliché and carries complex historical and cultural contexts that require deeper research. Based on their feedback, we moved the story into an ordinary house instead.

This shift made the horror more intimate and grounded: the setting feels familiar, which makes each anomaly more disturbing when it appears in a domestic space.

Level building & texturing

I arranged all the rooms in Unity—the living room, kitchen, hallway, basement, bedroom, and bathroom—by importing the 3D models and applying final materials to replace the white placeholders.

A teammate handled the lighting to create a subtly creepy yet natural atmosphere throughout the house. I modeled the living room, kitchen, basement, and hallway; my teammate modeled the bedroom and bathroom.

I textured the kitchen, hallway, and basement, using worn and aged surfaces to suggest both past habitation and neglect caused by mental illness. To enhance realism, I added details such as a blanket on the sofa and everyday kitchen items, learning many of these modeling techniques through YouTube tutorials.
